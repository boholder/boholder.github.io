Introduce to you: I

Hi, I am BoHolder. For the design of this virtual character and ID, go to here to view. I am not sure I can introduce myself in a short period of time, because I don't like to label myself or others. I think each individual is unique. So please allow me to blur myself as:

  • A craftsman who will do some small crafts when inspired;
  • An information collector who is happy to share information that complies with OSINT Features;
  • A mouse for tomorrow's cheese, actively changing its mode of action in order to adapt to new environments.

I am happy to make friends and learn from each other in free time, because a large number of weak links are more conducive to the individual's survival, in a variety of real-world environments.

About this site

I did it! finally...

As the website name shows, here is an output interface for me to share various things. From the beginning of junior high school, I thought about having a personal website to share the interesting comics I found. The way webpages are displayed and the technology is updated year by year, from the old pure HTML, to the current dynamic webpage, but building and maintaining the website seems still to be a big job. In addition, I like to act unobtrusively and reduce the attack surface for social engineering attack. Therefore, the personal website is also like the New Year's goal - delayed indefinitely.

Concealing your identity behind a handle is a juvenile and silly behavior characteristic of crackers, warez d00dz, and other lower life forms. Hackers don't do this; they're proud of what they do and want it associated with their real names. So if you have a handle, drop it. In the hacker culture it will only mark you as a loser.

Eric S. Raymond said the sentence above in his article How To Become A Hacker .

Oh, well, although the Internet environment in mainland China is different from the US, I don't want to be treated as loser, and I want to show my hand-made rough crafts to everyone proudly. Maybe this will inspire others about more valuable idea.

When I was involved in the translation of the Let's Encrypt Chinese documentation, I found the HUGO Website Framework , which blog posts are generated by converting from my favorite Markdown format. Looking through the documentation, I have an idea in my mind - that is it.

By the way, there is always something that you want to share with others, isn't it?

Chinese and English content synchronization

  • My native language is Chinese, so for casual content such as blogs, the preferred language is Chinese.
  • But if I make a new craft and want to show it to the Internet, then the preferred language becomes English. ((Most Google Translate + Little hand typing)*Polishing, Google Translate is a good time saving tool).

In summary, I will choose one language to update the new content as the case may be, and the version of the other language depends on the time and demand.

No comment system

Because it is a static website, there is no built-in comment system. Generally this is the API that uses Disqus, but it is a little troublesome to create an account. So I simply close the comment. If you want to tell me something, please send an issue or email.
